5 Signs Your Small Business Website Needs a Redesign

If your site is quietly costing you customers, it's usually one of these five problems.

2026-06-15 · Website · Little Technical Solutions LLC

A website doesn't have to be broken to be losing you business. Most of the sites we get called about still technically work — they just don't work hard enough. Here are five signs it's time for a redesign.

1. It doesn't work well on a phone

More than half of web traffic for most small businesses comes from phones, not desktops. If your site was built years ago and never properly adapted for mobile — text too small, buttons too close together, images that don't resize — visitors will leave before they ever read your pitch.

2. It's slow

If your homepage takes more than a few seconds to load, a real percentage of visitors are gone before they see it. Slow sites are usually the result of unoptimized images, outdated hosting, or bloated page builders — all fixable.

3. There's no clear call to action

A visitor should never have to hunt for how to contact you or request a quote. If your phone number, email, and a "get a quote" button aren't visible within a few seconds of landing on the page, you're losing leads to confusion, not competition.

4. It looks dated

Design trends move fast. Heavy drop shadows, stock photo clip art, auto-playing music, or a layout that hasn't changed since 2015 all quietly signal "this business might not be around anymore" — even if you're thriving.

5. You can't update it yourself (or it's terrifying to try)

If updating your hours, adding a new service, or swapping a photo means calling someone and waiting a week, that's a sign your site was built on the wrong platform for how you actually run your business.

None of these require starting from scratch. A redesign can often keep everything that's working and just fix what isn't — that's exactly what our Starter and Business website packages are built around.
